#### The Paralyzing Myths
![[Pasted image 20260709155710.png]]

**3 Wrong Mindsets/Myths about Discipleship**
1. Mentors
	- **Myth**: My pastor's primary role is to minister to me.  This causes church people to demand that pastors spoon feed them and meet all their spiritual needs.
	- **Truth**: **EQUIPPING**. A pastor's job is not primarily to minister to people but to equip people to minister to others. Life, church, and ministry are not primarily about the people in the pews. They are about God and others.

2. Ministry
	- **Myth**: I'm not yet ready to be used by God.  This convinces people they are too young, too old, have to many past sins, or simply don't know enough to engage in the work of the church.
	- **Truth**: **READINESS**. God is ready to use me now.  Even if they don't feel ready, are too young, or lost their temper yesterday and used a word the pastor would never use on Sunday - God wants to use them immediately.
>	Ministry is for the **MESSY**

3. Maturity
	- **Myth**: No one should minister until they are fully mature.  This creates a paralyzing chicken-and-egg conundrum.  Believers wait forever for a framed certificate of maturity before they even attempt to minister to others.
	- **Truth**: **THE GROWTH LOOP**. We will mature as we minister.  Which comes first? The Bible shows us that ministry drives maturity.  We can't wait until every believer feels mature enough to minister, because no one will mature until they minister.
