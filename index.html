Engage

Ptr. Manny — 1st Step in the Discipleship Journey

Relationship with God

Strategy: Make discipleship a way of life

Two Essential Dimensions

  • Holy Spirit — Disciple-making finds its true power through the empowerment of the Holy Spirit. (Acts 1:8)
  • Relational — Discipleship is relationship. True discipleship involves deep relationships. (John 13:34-35)
"Engaging someone begins the moment we start the conversation with someone that will eventually turn the conversation to sharing the gospel." — Jimmy Scroggins & Steve Wright
7 A woman from Samaria came to draw water. Jesus said to her, "Give me a drink." 8 (For his disciples had gone away into the city to buy food.) — John 4:7-8

Mindset and Method

Guiding Principles for Engaging Others Effectively

"You do not have to be interesting like someone dazzling, but you can be interested with people." — Dale Carnegie

To ENGAGE is to simply talk to people.

  • To show interest is to show importance
  • To engage is to ask diagnostic questions that encourage them to talk about themselves
  • To engage is to find a common interest or connection (music, hobby, sports)
  • Engaging is having the intention of befriending them
  • Be a living testimony to them
  • Goal: to invite them
  • Be of service to them in any way you can

James Engel Scale — Steps to Christ

Strategic Lessons — Method in Engaging

S.A.L.T. Method (Dr. Rice Broocks)

  • S — Start the conversation
  • A — Ask questions
  • L — Listen to them
  • T — Tell a story

To engage: Discipleship begins with relationships, not programs.

Tool: Best News Ever or Two Verse Evangelism

Recommended reading: Conspiracy of Kindness by Steve Sjogren

Biblical Principles on Engaging Others

1 After this the Lord appointed seventy-two others and sent them two by two ahead of him to every town and place where he was about to go. 2 He told them, "The harvest is plentiful, but the workers are few. Ask the Lord of the harvest, therefore, to send out workers into his harvest field." — Luke 10:1-2 (NIV)
  • You have a support team (para pag napasabak ka may back up)
  • You are well motivated (di ka tatamarin)
  • Ask the Lord to serve as engage team
  • Increase the numbers of workers
  • Mobilize workers to the harvest field

  2. Share your personal experience of how someone engaged you.

Step 2 Establish

Establish

Ptr. Manny — 2nd Step in the Discipleship Journey

Relationship with God
17 "Come, FOLLOW ME," Jesus said, "and I will send you out to fish for people." — Mark 1:17

Foundation is important as we disciple.

To Establish (verb)

  1. "To make firm or stable"
  2. "To introduce and cause to grow and multiply"

— Merriam-Webster Dictionary

Anything or anyone that is established can potentially grow and multiply. Therefore, when we establish people or a disciple to follow Jesus, the result is they continue to grow and multiply.

We must have a good facade AND good foundation. We must be rooted in Jesus and not rooted in personalities.

Strategic Process for Establishing a Disciple

Proving Question: Are they being established in the faith, or are they merely being Christianized?

11 For no one can lay any foundation other than the one already laid, which is Jesus Christ. — 1 Corinthians 3:11

Foundational Factors in Establishing a Disciple

1. Faith

  • By understanding salvation
  • Do they know how to depend on their faith?

2. By Establishing them in the Word and Prayer

6 And now, just as you accepted Christ Jesus as your Lord, you must continue to follow him. 7 Let your roots grow down into him, and let your lives be built on him. Then your faith will grow strong in the truth you were taught, and you will overflow with thankfulness.

8 Don't let anyone capture you with empty philosophies and high-sounding nonsense that come from human thinking and from the spiritual powers of this world, rather than from Christ. — Colossians 2:6-8
8 Keep this Book of the Law always on your lips; meditate on it day and night, so that you may be careful to do everything written in it. Then you will be prosperous and successful. — Joshua 1:8
  • Berean Jews listened eagerly and searched the Scriptures (Acts of the Apostles)
  • Lessons from Ezra's life: READ, APPLY AND SHARE
  • A.C.T.S. as a young believer (prayer method)
"What would sustain us from life's difficulty is the word of God." — Bishop Ferdie Cabiling

3. By Establishing them in the Church

  • Show them the value of the fellowship of the believers
  • Corporate worship
  • Being connected to a small group community is therapeutic

How to Grow Your Small Group?

Small group is just a catch basin — the goal is not the small group. The goal is discipleship.

"Small is Big. Big is small."
"Slow is fast." — Imagine the snowball effect (Law of Acceleration in Physics)

Biblical Strategy (Luke 10:2)

More leaders, more harvest. Less leaders, less harvest.

  • If you start small, you will grow big
  • Aim for all your small group members to become disciple-makers
  • Change the environment
  • Spiritual gifts should be discovered, developed, and fully used for God's purpose

To grow your disciples into leaders:

  • Show them how you do it
  • Give them opportunities to do what you do

The Performance-Driven Trap

The Ministry of Reconciliation

18 All this is from God, who reconciled us to himself through Christ and gave us the ministry of reconciliation: 19 that God was reconciling the world to himself in Christ, not counting people's sins against them. And he has committed to us the message of reconciliation. — 2 Corinthians 5:18-19

The Ephesians 4 Blueprint

The 3 Paralyzing Myths

Myth #1: Mentors

"My pastor's primary role is to minister to me." This causes church people to demand that pastors spoonfeed them and meet all their spiritual needs.

Truth #1: Equipping

A pastor's job is not primarily to minister to people but to equip people to minister to others. Life, church, and ministry are about God and others.

Myth #2: Ministry

"I'm not yet ready to be used by God." This convinces people they are too young, too old, have too many past sins, or simply don't know enough.

Truth #2: Readiness

God is ready to use me now. Even if they don't feel ready, are too young, or lost their temper yesterday — God wants to use them immediately. Ministry is for the messy.

Myth #3: Maturity

"No one should minister until they are fully mature." Believers wait forever for a framed certificate of maturity before they even attempt to minister.

Truth #3: The Growth Loop

We will mature as we minister. Ministry drives maturity. No one will mature until they minister.

The Equipping Shift

The Trap (Myth)The Blueprint (Truth)
Pastors spoonfeed me→Pastors equip me to minister.
I must be perfect and ready→God is ready to use me now.
Mature first, then serve→We mature by serving.

Tools / Environments

  • Victory Groups (Small groups, Life groups)
  • Making Disciples

Discussion Questions

  1. What excuse have you used in the past to prove that you weren't ready to minister?
  2. Which discipleship myths have you believed in the past? Why?
  3. Evaluate your current leadership development program. Do you believe in the next generation enough to place them in key roles in your church?
  4. How long does it take for you to make a member a minister or a key leader in your church?

Step 4 Empower

Empower

Ptr. Manny — 4th Step in the Discipleship Journey — Empowering Culture

Reaching out to others
17 And Jesus said to them, "Follow me, and I will make you become FISHERS OF MEN." — Mark 1:17

The Wikipedia Principle

  • Wiki — a Hawaiian word meaning "quick"
  • Nupedia — an online encyclopedia written by expert volunteers with a rigorous 7-step peer-review process. Slow.
  • Wikipedia — created by volunteers who can upload information quickly, making it immediately available to anyone.

Sometimes we as leaders can slow down the process of empowering people to minister and lead.

One Chapter Ahead

A disciple maker only needs to be ahead one chapter compared to who they disciple.

"The more we minister the more we will mature." — Ptr. Steve Murrell

When Do We Empower Others?

1. When the Foundation Has Been Laid

Acts 9:1-2 — Saul was breathing murderous threats to the disciples. Saul was giving approval for the killing of the saints.

Acts 9:4-8 — Saul encountered the Lord and was blinded and led by companion in Damascus.

Acts 9:9 — And for three days he was without sight, and neither ate nor drank.

Acts 9:20 — And immediately he proclaimed Jesus in the synagogues, saying, "He is the Son of God." — Acts 9

Discipleship is not about perfection but about direction.

Acts 9:10-15 — Now there was a believer in Damascus named Ananias. The Lord spoke to him in a vision, calling, "Ananias!" "Yes, Lord!" he replied. The Lord said, "Go over to Straight Street, to the house of Judas. When you get there, ask for a man from Tarsus named Saul. He is praying to me right now."

But Lord," exclaimed Ananias, "I've heard many people talk about the terrible things this man has done to the believers in Jerusalem!" ... But the Lord said, "Go, for Saul is my chosen instrument to take my message to the Gentiles and to kings, as well as to the people of Israel." — Acts 9:10-15

2. Immediately

And immediately he proclaimed Jesus in the synagogues, saying, "He is the Son of God." (Acts 9:20)

The 3 Strong Foundations (Acts 2:38)

  1. Repentance
  2. Water Baptism
  3. Baptism in the Holy Spirit

Results of Empowering Culture

1. Spiritual Strength is the Result of Doing Ministry

Acts 9:22 — But Saul increased all the more in strength and confounded the Jews who lived in Damascus by proving that Jesus was the Christ. — Acts 9:22
  • Strength in ministry comes from empowering others
  • Creating or providing a platform for ministry unleashes innovation that serves as spiritual strength for someone
  • A strong disciple is formed by empowering, not spoon-feeding

2. Strong Relational Connections

Acts 9:23-25 — When many days had passed, the Jews plotted to kill him, but their plot became known to Saul. They were watching the gates day and night in order to kill him, but his disciples took him by night and let him down through an opening in the wall, lowering him in a basket. — Acts 9:23-25

We may never be like Paul, but we can hope and pray to be the ABC — easy to someone:

  • A — Ananias (Acts 9:10 — "Here I am, Lord.")
  • B — Barnabas — He vouched for Paul to the Apostles. They were co-workers in Antioch.
  • C — Church (Acts 9:25 — His disciples took him by night and let him down through the wall in a basket.)
So the church throughout all Judea and Galilee and Samaria had peace and was being built up. And walking in the fear of the Lord and in the comfort of the Holy Spirit, it multiplied. — Acts 9:31
